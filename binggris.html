LHO Afinda Zulia

LHO Text about Fabric Waste Waste is a residual material that is not used from an industrial activity that has the potential to pollute the environment and have a negative impact on health if not managed properly. Waste can be in solid, liquid, and gas forms and is differentiated into organic and inorganic. In this observation, I will explain several things about fabric scraps. As I know, fabric scraps are unused fabric waste collected from various sources such as tailors, textile factories, and convection industries. Fabric scraps are flammable and usually come from leftover rayon, linen, wool, and so on. Fabric scraps that have been selected are then cleaned of dirt, dust, and thread remnants. The cleaning process can be done manually or using a machine. Usually, fabric scraps are cut into small pieces first before being shredded into fine fibers. These fibers can then be used as raw materials to make new products. Fabric scraps are processed into products that have high selling value. One of the crafts that can be made from fabric scraps is a tote bag. By producing this, it can reduce environmental pollution, reduce the amount of waste dumped into rivers, create economic value, conserve natural resources, and increase environmental awareness. Fabric scrap management can be an example of environmentally friendly practices that can inspire people to be more concerned about the environment. Fabric scrap waste management is an effective and creative solution to address textile waste problems through the process of collection, selection, cleaning, management, and finishing. Therefore, support and participation from various parties are needed to develop fabric scrap management.

LHO Lintan Sekar

Patchwork Fabric Waste I would like to present the observational report I conducted a few days ago. The object of my observation was patchwork fabric waste. Previously, waste is defined as leftover materials or by-products from the production of an item that are not used. Waste can damage environmental health if it is not properly treated. Based on my observations, I will explain about patchwork fabric waste. Patchwork fabric consists of leftover pieces of cloth from the textile industry and clothing production. Patchwork fabric can be processed into various new and interesting products. It comes from different types of fabric, such as cotton and silk. Patchwork fabric can be an environmentally friendly material for making crafts. It can also be an appealing material for creating new and unique items. With creativity, this fabric can be transformed into wallets, bags, and home decorations. The colors of patchwork fabric are very diverse because they come from various types of fabric. Some pieces are white, colorful, plain, or patterned. The patterns also vary, such as floral, batik, and camouflage. The sizes of patchwork pieces differ depending on the crafts or products to be made. However, patchwork pieces often come in irregular and uneven sizes, such as small and random cuts. These uneven sizes can be both a challenge and an opportunity for creativity when working with patchwork fabric. Patchwork fabric can be used to create various unique and creative handicrafts. It is also beneficial in reducing plastic waste, as patchwork fabric is a type of waste that decomposes slowly. Patchwork fabric can provide economic value if processed into crafts. Making handicrafts from patchwork fabric helps develop sewing, designing, and creative skills. Therefore, patchwork fabric can be a useful and creative material for making various handmade crafts.

LHO Nanda Yesika Bela S.[24]

Waste is a material or residual substance produced by consumption and production activities that does not have a high sales value. However, waste can be processed properly and have a positive impact on the environment, for example, reducing environmental pollution. Waste can also be recycled and resold at a fairly affordable price. Cloth scraps are waste from leftover fabric or scraps of fabric that can be reused to make crafts or products. These scraps are commonly used to make crafts such as bags, key chains, and so on. Cloth scraps typically come in the form of small pieces of fabric and abstract shapes. They have a soft texture and are slightly rough. Cloth scraps offer experimentation because of their different shapes, sizes, colors, textures, and patterns. These differences allow us to create something unique. Furthermore, using scraps can also reduce waste and save costs. Using scraps of fabric allows us to create marketable products. In addition, we can also reduce environmental pollution. Scrap fabric is not just a scrap of fabric but can also be a valuable material for creating new and creative crafts or products.
